## 2. Completed Discoveries and Conclusions

### Discovery 1: Policy-as-Code comes from Liquid Pass

The concept of deterministic, unbreakable policies that govern agent decisions is not theoretical — it's a direct evolution of the credit decision engine (Liquid Pass). The v2 rewrite with DDD/Hexagonal architecture and 12 types of decision graph nodes provides the technical foundation for the Policy Builder concept in the new platform.

**Conclusion:** The Policy Builder no-code concept is grounded in real, production-tested technology. The challenge is evolving it from a credit-specific engine to a general-purpose policy system that can serve multiple use cases while maintaining determinism.

### Discovery 2: Repasse operations are the clearest path to liquidity

Soma Repasse has demonstrated that the full repasse cycle (from initial buyer contact to registry and settlement) can be managed systematically. With ~962 contracts and relationships with major incorporadoras and banks, this is the most validated liquidity operation.

**Conclusion:** Repasse is the beachhead for liquidity. The operational knowledge, stakeholder relationships, and process understanding from Soma should be the first workflow to be "agentified" in the new platform.

### Discovery 3: Portfolio transactions are already happening

Transação Carteira demonstrates that Liquid has the capability and positioning to intermediate portfolio-level transactions, even without the unified platform.

**Conclusion:** The liquidity thesis is not just aspirational — it's being validated by real transactions. The platform needs to formalize and scale what's already happening manually.

### Discovery 4: Multi-bureau integration is a solved problem

Liquid Pass already integrates with Serasa, Equifax, PH3A, and Open Finance. The connector architecture for bureau data is established.

**Conclusion:** Connectors for credit data are a known capability. The challenge moves from "can we connect?" to "how do we make agents use these connections intelligently?"

### Discovery 5: The market responds to domain expertise, not generic AI

Through Soma's client relationships and Liquid Pass adoption, the company has validated that real estate credit stakeholders value domain-specific intelligence over generic AI capabilities.

**Conclusion:** The differentiation strategy (depth in regulated market vs. horizontal AI) has market evidence supporting it.

## 3. Mistakes Made and Lessons Learned

### Lesson 1: Isolated products create fragmentation

Liquid Pass, Liquid Play, and Soma operate as separate products with different tech stacks, different interfaces, and different data models. This fragmentation:

- Increases maintenance cost
- Prevents cross-product intelligence
- Confuses the market positioning
- Makes it impossible to build the liquidity funnel

**Learning:** The new platform must be unified from day one. No new isolated products.

### Lesson 2: Operations without platform = manual scaling

Soma Repasse operates via [Monday.com](http://Monday.com) CRM with human analysts. This works at ~962 contracts but doesn't scale. Every new contract requires proportional human effort.

**Learning:** Agent-assisted operations are not optional — they're the only way to scale liquidity operations without linear headcount growth.

### Lesson 3: v1 technical debt constrains evolution

Liquid Pass v1 is legacy active with clients but architecturally constrained. The decision to rewrite (v2) acknowledges that the original architecture cannot support the agentic platform vision.

**Learning:** Architecture decisions made at the start persist. The new platform must be designed for the long-term vision, not just the immediate use case. This is why the non-negotiable principles exist.

### Lesson 4: Thinking in "pieces" instead of "system"

The CEO braindump explicitly acknowledges this: "peças muito soltas" (very loose pieces). Months of thinking without documentation created a situation where the vision exists in one person's head but isn't shared with the team.

**Learning:** Continuous documentation and alignment is not overhead — it's infrastructure. This Context Map project is the correction.
