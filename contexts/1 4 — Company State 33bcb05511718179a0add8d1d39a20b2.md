## 1. Team: Composition, Seniority, Gaps

### Current Organizational Model

The braindump and brain dumps describe a transition from a traditional team to a new organizational model built around the "machine" (the agentic platform). The CEO's vision:

- **YOS Engineers / Developers:** Build and operate the machine itself (platform infrastructure, CI/CD, systems)
- **Context Engineers:** Prepare the creation base, structure product context, organize feedback from agent responses. This includes researchers, organizers, instructors, and HQAs (High-Quality Assessors)
- **The human company is built around the machine, not the other way around.** The company must not depend on human processes at every step from product to billing.

### Known Team Components

| Area | Known Personnel/Capacity | Gap Assessment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **CEO/Founder** | Active — product vision, strategy, market relationships | Key-person dependency risk — vision concentrated in one person |
| **Soma Operations** | Dedicated analyst team for repasse operations | Operational capacity exists but manual; needs productization |
| **Development** | Active team (working on Liquid Pass v2 rewrite) | Unclear if team has experience with agent architectures, MCP, orchestration |
| **Gil (mentioned in braindump)** | Built dashboard/Data Blast prototype | Unclear current status; context about what was built needs documentation |
| **Sócio (partner)** | Mentioned as having "global principles" | Not documented; principles not listed — alignment gap |

### Critical Gaps Identified

1. **Data Architect:** Needed for entity resolution, domain model design, cross-tenant data architecture
2. **Agent Architecture Specialist:** Experience with MCP, A2A, multi-agent orchestration
3. **Legal/Compliance:** No mention of internal legal counsel; regulatory mapping absent
4. **Growth/Marketing:** PLG requires dedicated growth expertise
5. **Design/UX:** Cognitive architecture for the new platform needs design leadership
6. **DPO (Data Protection Officer):** Required for LGPD compliance, especially with entity-unique-in-root architecture

## 6. Internal Risks

### Key-Person Dependency

- **CEO/Founder:** Vision, strategy, and market relationships concentrated in one person. The braindump explicitly represents months of undocumented thinking. Risk: if CEO is unavailable, the company loses its strategic compass.
- **Sócio (Partner):** Mentioned but principles not documented. Potential misalignment risk.

### Technical Debt

- **Liquid Pass v1:** Legacy codebase with active clients. Must maintain while building v2.
- **Soma on [Monday.com](http://Monday.com):** Manual CRM operations that need productization. Operational debt.
- **Multiple codebases:** Different products in different stacks create fragmentation.

### Revenue Concentration

- Small client base means high impact of any single client loss
- Revenue likely below self-sustaining threshold

### Knowledge Concentration

- Operational process knowledge (repasse) concentrated in Soma team
- Technical knowledge split between different product teams
- Strategic knowledge concentrated in CEO

### Execution Capacity

- The vision described in the braindump (12+ entities, 4 agent layers, 2 platform sides, global architecture, PLG, compliance) represents a roadmap of 3+ years compressed into a single vision statement
- Team size vs. ambition gap is the primary execution risk

## 7. Known Bottlenecks

1. **Documentation gap:** Months of strategic thinking undocumented until now. Team cannot execute what isn't shared.
2. **Architecture transition:** Moving from isolated products to unified platform while maintaining production services.
3. **Entity model undefined:** The most critical data architecture decision (how to model entities, inventories, and entity resolution) is still open.
4. **Regulatory mapping absent:** Operating in "regulated market" without specific regulatory requirements mapped.
5. **Financial runway:** Implied pressure to generate revenue quickly, constraining long-term architectural investments.
6. **Hiring:** Key roles (data architect, agent specialist, legal, growth) are gaps that block progress on critical decisions.
