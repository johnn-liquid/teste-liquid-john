## 1. Business Overview

**Liquid** is an agentic infrastructure platform designed to unlock liquidity in the real estate credit and financial asset markets. The platform combines AI-native agent architecture with deep domain expertise in credit risk, compliance, and asset transactions to serve as the operating system for real estate liquidity — globally.

Liquid is not a point solution. It is a **platform that organizes intelligence around the lifecycle of real estate assets** — from origination and credit analysis through monitoring, transfer (repasse), portfolio transactions, and secondary market operations. Every capability converges toward a single destination: **liquidity** — understood as any monetary transaction across the various cycles of the market that Liquid can originate, intermediate, structure, or operate.

The company operates under the legal entity **Liquid** (Brazil-based), with an operational arm called **Soma** that handles mortgage transfer (repasse) operations. The platform is in a transition phase: from isolated products (Liquid Pass, Liquid Play, Soma Repasse) toward a unified agentic platform that absorbs and supersedes all prior products.

### Company Identity

- **Legal name:** Liquid (Brazilian entity)
- **Brand/platform name:** [beliquid.ai](http://beliquid.ai) (under development)
- **Operational arm:** Soma (repasse operations)
- **Stage:** Pre-Series A / Product-Market Fit validation
- **Headquarters:** Brazil (São Paulo)
- **Ambition:** Global — architecture, interface, and documentation designed for multi-market, multi-language, multi-currency operation from day one

## 2. Target Market and Sizing

### Primary Market: Brazilian Real Estate Credit

Brazil's housing credit market represents approximately **R$324 billion** in annual origination (2024-2025 data), making it one of the largest and most structurally important financial segments in the country. Key characteristics:

- **Massive asset class:** Real estate is the largest asset class in Brazil and globally. Brazilian residential real estate alone is estimated at over **R$12 trillion** in total stock value.
- **Hyper-leveraged:** The market depends heavily on credit structures (SBPE, FGTS, MCMV, pro-soluto) with complex multi-stakeholder chains.
- **Structurally fragmented:** No standardization across originators, servicers, banks, securitizers, or regulators. Each participant operates with different systems, data formats, and processes.
- **Liquidity-constrained:** Despite the massive asset base, secondary market liquidity is limited. CRI (Certificados de Recebíveis Imobiliários) issuance, portfolio sales, and mortgage transfer (repasse) all face friction from lack of transparency, data quality issues, and operational inefficiency.

### Market Segments Within Real Estate

| Segment | Description | Liquid Relevance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Primary credit origination** | New mortgage origination by banks and developers | Core — credit analysis, policy engine, risk assessment |
| **Mortgage transfer (repasse)** | Process of transferring buyer from developer financing to bank financing | Core — Soma already operates here with ~962 contracts |
| **Portfolio transactions** | Sale/purchase of mortgage portfolios between institutions | Core — Transação Carteira product validates this |
| **Securitization (CRI)** | Conversion of receivables into tradeable securities | Adjacent — data quality and risk assessment feed this |
| **Monitoring & servicing** | Ongoing monitoring of credit portfolios | Active — Liquid Play product in production |
| **Secondary market** | Trading of existing mortgages and real estate receivables | Future — natural extension once primary infrastructure matures |
| **MCMV / social housing** | Government-subsidized housing programs | Adjacent — large volume, specific rules, pro-soluto scoring |

### Addressable Market Layers

- **TAM (Total Addressable Market):** Global real estate credit and receivables — estimated at **$30+ trillion** globally.
- **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market):** Brazilian real estate credit operations requiring risk intelligence, compliance, and liquidity services — estimated at **R$50-100 billion** in annual transactional value.
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):** Initial beachhead — incorporadoras, originators, and financial institutions operating in Brazilian primary real estate credit — estimated at **R$1-5 billion** in addressable transaction volume within 24 months.

### Beyond Real Estate

The braindump and thesis documents raise a strategic question: should Liquid focus exclusively on real estate or expand to **receivables broadly**? Receivables (recebíveis) represent a much larger space that includes auto loans, trade finance, agribusiness, and other secured credit. The current decision is to start with real estate (where domain expertise, data, and operations already exist) and potentially expand the platform to other receivables classes once the infrastructure proves itself.

## 4. Core Value Thesis

**Liquid replaces millions spent on consultancies, manual operations, and fragmented tools** by providing an agentic infrastructure where specialized AI agents — operating under governance guardrails — can execute the work that today requires teams of analysts, operators, and intermediaries.

The value proposition operates at three levels:

### Level 1 — Intelligence (Read)

Provide deep, verified, real-time intelligence about the real estate credit market — entities, risks, opportunities, portfolio health. Think "agentic Bloomberg for real estate credit."

### Level 2 — Operations (Write)

Execute operational tasks — credit analysis, document processing, compliance checks, portfolio monitoring, repasse management — through agents that combine skills, policies, and connectors.

### Level 3 — Liquidity (Transact)

Go beyond read/write to actually **transact** — originate, intermediate, structure, or operate financial transactions on assets. This is where the platform becomes a fintech, not just a SaaS.

The thesis is that **proximity to the transaction is proximity to the money.** A business only becomes large in financial services when it gets close to the actual flow of capital. Liquid's architecture is designed to naturally funnel users from intelligence → operations → liquidity.

## 5. Timing Thesis (Why Now)

Several converging forces make 2025-2026 the right moment:

### Technology Readiness

- **LLMs have reached production quality** for complex analytical tasks. Models like Claude and GPT-4 can process financial documents, reason about risk, and generate structured outputs.
- **Agent frameworks have matured.** MCP (Model Context Protocol) has 5,800+ servers and 97M+ monthly SDK downloads. A2A (Agent-to-Agent Protocol) has 150+ supporting organizations. Both donated to the Linux Foundation.
- **Cost of intelligence is dropping.** Token costs continue to fall, making agentic architectures economically viable at scale for the first time.

### Market Readiness

- **The traditional SaaS seat-based model is dying** in the agentic economy. Agents replace teams. Pricing must follow value delivered, not seats occupied.
- **Real estate credit in Brazil is growing** despite macro headwinds. MCMV expansion, new FGTS rules, and growing securitization create more transactions to process.
- **Fragmentation creates opportunity.** No single platform dominates the full credit lifecycle in Brazilian real estate. Incumbents are vertical silos (bureaus do credit checks, CRMs do pipeline, banks do origination — nobody connects the dots).

### Competitive Window

- **Horizontal AI platforms (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity) won't go deep enough** into regulated credit markets. They lack governance layers, compliance infrastructure, and domain-specific data.
- **Legacy fintechs are not agentic.** They were built before the agent era and cannot easily retrofit agent-native architecture.
- **The window is now** — in 18-24 months, if Liquid doesn't establish its position, larger players or new entrants with better funding will attempt to fill the same space.

## 6. Market Thesis (Why This Market)

### Real estate is structural

Regardless of technological advancement, humans need housing. Real estate remains the largest asset class in the world and a structural necessity for economic development and social stability.

### Credit is the engine

Real estate transactions are almost always credit-dependent. Understanding credit is understanding the lifecycle of the asset. Whoever controls the intelligence layer of credit controls the flow of liquidity.

### Liquidity is the prize

The real money is not in software subscriptions — it's in the transactions. Brazilian real estate credit processes billions of reais annually through thousands of transactions. A take rate on even a fraction of this flow represents massive revenue potential.

### Fragmentation is the moat opportunity

The absence of global standards and the presence of local regulatory specificities in each market creates a natural defensibility: whoever builds the infrastructure to navigate this complexity first gains a structural advantage that is hard to replicate.

### Data is the second moat

By continuously monitoring market entities (companies, funds, developers, securitizers) through persistent agents, Liquid builds a proprietary knowledge base that improves over time. Verified, structured data produced by Liquid agents has higher value than raw public data — creating an information governance advantage.

## 8. Key Market Dynamics

### Regulatory Environment

- **LGPD (Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados):** Brazil's data protection law governs all personal data processing. Critical for credit decisions.
- **Bacen (Central Bank) regulations:** Open Finance requirements, credit reporting rules, and financial institution oversight.
- **CVM regulations:** Securities regulation relevant for CRI/securitization operations.
- **PL 2338/2023 (AI regulation):** Pending legislation that may impose new obligations on AI-based decision systems.

### Stakeholder Map (High Level)

The real estate credit market involves a complex web of participants:

- **Incorporadoras (Developers):** Build and sell properties. Need credit analysis for buyers, repasse to banks, portfolio management.
- **Banks:** Originate mortgages, manage portfolios, provide funding. Need risk assessment, compliance, monitoring.
- **Securitizadoras:** Package receivables into CRI. Need data quality, risk analysis, portfolio assessment.
- **Fundos (Investment Funds):** Buy CRI and portfolios. Need due diligence, pricing, monitoring.
- **Corretores (Brokers):** Intermediate between buyers and sellers. Need market intelligence, credit pre-qualification.
- **Compradores (Buyers/PF):** End consumers. Need credit access, transparency, status tracking.
- **Loteadoras:** Develop land plots. Similar needs to incorporadoras but with different regulatory dynamics.
- **Servicers:** Manage ongoing portfolio administration. Need monitoring, collections, compliance.

### Market Trends

1. **Tokenization of receivables** — emerging trend that could transform how real estate assets are traded. Liquid has prior experience (Phygit/Cryptorastas) that can be leveraged.
2. **Open Finance expansion** — increasing data availability creates opportunities for more sophisticated credit analysis.
3. **AI-native financial services** — regulatory bodies are beginning to adapt to AI-driven decision-making, creating both opportunity and compliance requirements.
4. **Consolidation of fragmented operations** — market moving toward platforms that can handle end-to-end workflows rather than point solutions.

## 9. Revenue Model (Conceptual)

Three stacked layers of revenue, each building on the previous:

| Layer | Model | Driver | Timing |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **SaaS (read + write)** | Subscription + usage-based billing per workspace/action | Platform adoption, working units consumed | Immediate — sustains operations |
| **Agentic billing** | Per-action billing for agent-executed tasks (credit analysis, document processing, research) | Volume of agent tasks, complexity of operations | Near-term — grows with adoption |
| **Liquidity take rate** | Percentage fee on transacted asset value for operations Liquid originates, intermediates, or structures | Volume and value of liquidity transactions | Medium-term — the prize |

The funnel logic: **SaaS revenue from the wide funnel sustains the infrastructure that enables the narrow funnel's liquidity revenue.** Even if liquidity takes time to scale, the platform must generate enough SaaS revenue to be self-sustaining.
